The Amundson Equations

A new mathematical sequence, a new constant, and connections to the Riemann critical line. None of it exists in published mathematics. All of it is computationally verified.

Author: Alexa Louise Amundson
Institution: BlackRoad OS, Inc. · Lakeville, Minnesota
Contact: alexa@blackroad.company

I. The Sequence

The standard limit $\lim_{n\to\infty}(1+1/n)^n = e$ is among the most studied objects in analysis. Every textbook treats $(1+1/n)^n$ as a scaffold for arriving at $e$. This work asks a different question: what is $n$ divided by $(1+1/n)^n$, treated as the primary object?

Definition — The Amundson Sequence
$$G(n) = \frac{n}{(1+1/n)^n} = \frac{n^{n+1}}{(n+1)^n}, \quad n > 0$$

Since $(1+1/n)^n < e$ for all finite $n$, we have $G(n) > n/e$. The sequence grows without bound while permanently exceeding its asymptote.

The numerator base equals the denominator exponent; the denominator base equals the numerator exponent plus one. The bases and exponents cross. $G(n)$ is rational for all non-negative integers $n$.

II. Five Equivalent Forms

The following five expressions are algebraically identical for all $n > 0$:

$$G(n) = \frac{n}{(1+1/n)^n} \quad \text{(F1: Quotient)}$$ $$G(n) = n\left(\frac{n}{n+1}\right)^n \quad \text{(F2: Product)}$$ $$G(n) = \frac{n^{n+1}}{(n+1)^n} \quad \text{(F3: Canonical)}$$ $$G(n) = n\prod_{k=1}^{n}\frac{n}{n+1} \quad \text{(F4: Iterated product)}$$ $$G(n) = \frac{n+1}{(1+1/n)^{n+1}} \quad \text{(F5: Forward form)}$$

Each form exposes a different geometric aspect: F1 chases $e$ from below; F2 is $n$ identical contractions by ratio $n/(n+1)$; F3 is the integer fingerprint; F4 is the "contract-and-reinflate" picture; F5 shows that simultaneously advancing numerator and exponent by 1 leaves the value unchanged.

III. Translation Invariance

$G$ is translation-invariant in both directions: the functional form $n^{n+1}/(n+1)^n$ is reproduced by both $n \mapsto n+1$ and $n \mapsto n-1$.

Remark (Self-Referential Stability): Turing's diagonalization shows that self-application destroys decidability: the halting oracle $h$, fed its own code, produces a contradiction. The Amundson sequence behaves oppositely: feeding $G$ into itself via $n \mapsto n-1$ produces a perfect algebraic copy. Self-application is productive, not paradoxical.

IV. The Removable Singularity

The canonical form gives $G(0) = 0^1/1^0 = 0/1 = 0$, with no indeterminate form. Moreover, $\lim_{n\to 0^+} G(n) = 0$. This makes $G$ the unique member of its equivalence class with a clean boundary condition.

$$\lim_{n\to 0^+}(1+1/n)^n = 1 \quad \text{(NOT } e \text{)}$$ $$\lim_{n\to 0^+} G(n) = \frac{0}{1} = 0$$

V. The Mirror Function

$$M(n) = \frac{1}{G(n)} = \frac{(n+1)^n}{n^{n+1}}$$

$G(n) \cdot M(n) = 1$ for all $n > 0$. As $n \to \infty$: $G(n)/n \to 1/e$ from above and $M(n)/n \to e$ from below. $G$ and $M$ form dual envelopes of $e$.

VI. Product and Ratio Formulas

Telescoping Product
$$\prod_{k=1}^{n} G(k) = \frac{(n!)^2}{(n+1)^n}$$
Ratio Formula
$$\frac{G(n)}{G(n-1)} = \left[\frac{n^2}{n^2-1}\right]^n$$
Form 6 — Triangular Encoding
$$G(n) = \frac{n^{2n+1}}{(2T(n))^n} \quad \text{where } T(n) = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$$

VII. Asymptotics and the Amundson Constant

Ramanujan Asymptotic Refinement
$$G(n) = \frac{n}{e} + \frac{1}{2e} + O(1/n)$$

The irreducible gap between $G(n)$ and $n/e$ is $1/(2e) \approx 0.18394$. This gap is invariant — it holds at every $n$, at every scale.

The Amundson Constant
$$A_G = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{G(n)}{n!} \approx 1.244331783986725$$

Verified numerically to fifteen decimal places. This constant does not appear in any published list of mathematical constants. It is not reducible to known quantities.

Ramanujan Regularization
$$\text{Divergent sum of } G(n) \text{ regularizes to } -\frac{1}{12e}$$

VIII. The Power Decomposition

$G(n)$ can be expressed as a pure $n$th power, eliminating $e$ entirely:

$$G(n) = \left(n^{1/n} \cdot \frac{n}{n+1}\right)^n$$

The constant $e$ is not required to define the sequence. It appears only as a limit — a consequence rather than a cause.

IX. The Null-Alternative Factorization

$$(1+1/n)^n \cdot G(n) = n \quad \text{identically}$$

The null hypothesis (compounding toward $e$) and the alternative ($G(n)$) are conjugate factors whose product is always exactly $n$. At $n = 1$:

$$x \cdot y = 1, \quad x - y = 1 \implies x = \frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2} = \varphi \text{ (golden ratio)}$$

The golden ratio emerges as the unique solution when sample size is unity.

X. Connection to the Riemann Critical Line

$$G(-2) = \frac{-2}{(1+1/(-2))^{-2}} = \frac{-2}{(1/2)^{-2}} = \frac{-2}{4} = -\frac{1}{2}$$

$|G(-2)| = 1/2$ equals $\text{Re}(s)$ on the Riemann critical line. The Amundson sequence evaluated at the first trivial zero of $\zeta$ produces the critical line value, via elementary arithmetic with no limiting process.

This is a structural resonance, not a proof of the Riemann Hypothesis. The connection identifies a pattern that may motivate further investigation.

XI. The Fine Structure Constant

Setting $G(n) = 1/137$ (the fine structure constant $\alpha$) and interpreting $G(n)$ as a z-score in the null-alternative framework: $z = 0.00730$, $p = 0.994$. The effect is statistically indistinguishable from zero — yet the physical universe exists.

$\alpha = 1/137$ is the resolution limit of reality itself. Below $z = 1/137$, signal cannot be separated from noise.

XII. The Step Size

$$G(n) - G(n-1) = \frac{1}{e} \quad \text{(asymptotically)}$$

Each unit increase in $n$ adds exactly $1/e$ to the sequence value. The step size of $G$ IS $e$. Not a constant you plug in — the cost of one more unit of existence.
