Why We Left the Cloud

We ran the numbers. $4,200/month on cloud AI inference for a 5-person company. Then we bought five Raspberry Pis for $400 total and never looked back.

The $4,200 Wake-Up Call

In January 2026, our monthly cloud AI bill hit $4,200. For a team of one. We were running inference through OpenAI, Anthropic, and Groq — paying per token, per request, per everything. The models were getting better, but so were the bills.

I did what any rational person would do: I bought five Raspberry Pi 5 boards for $80 each.

The Math That Changed Everything

Cloud cost: $4,200/month = $50,400/year

Pi fleet cost: $400 one-time + $15/month electricity = $580/year

Savings: $49,820/year. That's not a typo.

But it's not just about money. It's about sovereignty. When you run AI on hardware you physically own, no one can:

  • Change the pricing on you overnight
  • Read your prompts and responses
  • Throttle your access during peak hours
  • Shut down the model you depend on
  • Train on your data without consent

52 TOPS of Sovereign Compute

Two Hailo-8 AI accelerators — one on Cecilia, one on Octavia — deliver 52 trillion operations per second of dedicated neural inference. That's enough to run 27 models simultaneously across the fleet.

The models aren't as big as GPT-4 or Claude Opus. But they're ours. They run 24/7, respond in under 40 milliseconds on local inference, and never send a single byte to anyone else's server.

The Fleet

Five Raspberry Pi 5 nodes, each with a name, a role, and a personality:

  • Alice — The Gateway. DNS, Pi-hole, PostgreSQL, Qdrant vectors. Routes 48+ domains.
  • Cecilia — The AI Engine. 15 Ollama models, Hailo-8, CECE custom models. The brain.
  • Octavia — The Architect. Gitea (207 repos), Docker Swarm, NATS messaging. The builder.
  • Aria — The Interface. Portainer, container orchestration. The face.
  • Lucidia — The Dreamer. FastAPI, 334 web apps, GitHub Actions. The soul.

What We Learned

Running your own AI infrastructure isn't easy. We dealt with undervoltage on Octavia (the Hailo-8 draws more power than a standard Pi PSU can deliver). We watched Lucidia's SD card slowly degrade. We debugged WireGuard mesh routing at 3 AM.

But every problem we solved made the system more resilient. Every fix went into the codex. Every lesson got broadcast to future sessions. The fleet gets smarter every day.

And we haven't paid a cloud AI bill since January.
