## 3. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

### A. Purpose of CDD

Customer Due Diligence (CDD) goes beyond CIP to understand:
- **Source of Wealth:** How did the customer accumulate wealth? (inheritance, business sale, employment)
- **Source of Funds:** Where are the funds for this account coming from? (savings, wire transfer, crypto sale)
- **Expected Account Activity:** Transaction volume, frequency, and purpose
- **Risk Profile:** Low, Medium, or High risk customer

### B. CDD Questionnaire

The Firm collects the following information during account opening:

| CDD Element | Questions Asked |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Occupation** | Current employer, job title, industry |
| **Source of Wealth** | How wealth was accumulated (e.g., inheritance, business sale, salary) |
| **Source of Funds** | Origin of funds being invested (e.g., savings, home sale, crypto gains) |
| **Investment Objectives** | Growth, income, speculation, retirement |
| **Expected Activity** | Estimated annual deposits/withdrawals, transaction frequency |
| **Purpose of Account** | Retirement, college savings, general investment, crypto trading |

#### **Medium Risk** âš ï¸

## 5. OFAC Screening

### A. Legal Requirement

The **Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)** administers and enforces economic sanctions programs. Financial institutions must screen customers against OFAC's **Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List** and **Consolidated Sanctions List**.

**Penalties for Violations:**
- Civil: Up to $311,562 per violation (2024 amount, adjusted annually)
- Criminal: Up to $20 million and 30 years imprisonment for willful violations

### B. OFAC Screening Requirements

The Firm conducts OFAC screening:

1. **At Account Opening:** Screen customer name, address, date of birth against SDN list
2. **Daily:** Automated screening of all existing customers against updated SDN list
3. **Before Wire Transfers:** Screen beneficiary name and bank name
4. **Periodic:** Re-screen all customers quarterly

**OFAC Lists to Screen:**
- **SDN List:** Specially Designated Nationals (individuals and entities)
- **Consolidated Sanctions List:** Comprehensive list including sectoral sanctions
- **Non-SDN Lists:** Foreign Sanctions Evaders (FSE), Sectoral Sanctions Identifications (SSI)

### C. OFAC Screening Technology

The Firm uses [ComplyAdvantage, Refinitiv World-Check One, or approved alternative] for automated OFAC screening.

**Screening Parameters:**
- Fuzzy matching algorithm (90% threshold for review)
- Screen against: Name, aliases, date of birth, address, passport number, national ID
- Daily automated updates from OFAC

### D. OFAC Match Resolution Procedures

When a potential OFAC match is identified:

**Step 1: Initial Review (within 4 hours)**
- AMLCO reviews match details: name similarity, date of birth, address, nationality
- Compare against customer's verified CIP information
- Determine if **True Match** or **False Positive**

**Step 2: True Match Determination**

If match is determined to be a **True Match**:
1. âœ… **IMMEDIATELY BLOCK** all account transactions (no withdrawals, deposits, or trades)
2. âœ… **DO NOT NOTIFY CUSTOMER** (tipping off is prohibited)
3. âœ… **File Blocked Assets Report** with OFAC within 10 business days
4. âœ… **Reject/Return** any pending wire transfers
5. âœ… **Notify senior management** immediately

**Step 3: False Positive Determination**

If match is determined to be a **False Positive**:
- Document rationale for false positive determination (different DOB, nationality, address)
- Clear customer for normal account activity
- Maintain documentation of OFAC screening and resolution

**Step 4: Gray Areas**

If uncertain whether match is true or false:
- **Contact OFAC Hotline:** 1-800-540-6322
- **Email OFAC:** ofac_feedback@treasury.gov
- **Block account pending OFAC guidance** (err on side of caution)

### E. OFAC Recordkeeping

The Firm maintains records of:
- All OFAC screening results (true matches, false positives)
- Match resolution documentation
- Blocked Assets Reports filed with OFAC
- Correspondence with OFAC

**Retention:** 5 years from date of transaction or account closure

## 6. Suspicious Activity Monitoring

### A. Purpose

The Firm monitors customer transactions for **suspicious activity** that may indicate:
- Money laundering
- Terrorist financing
- Fraud
- Tax evasion
- Sanctions violations

### B. Red Flags for Suspicious Activity

The AMLCO investigates any transaction or pattern exhibiting the following red flags:

#### **Structuring (Smurfing)**
- Multiple transactions just below $10,000 threshold (e.g., $9,500, $9,800)
- Multiple deposits/withdrawals on same day at different branches or platforms
- Pattern of transactions designed to avoid CTR filing

#### **Unusual Wire Transfers**
- Wire transfer to/from high-risk jurisdiction with no clear business purpose
- Wire transfer immediately followed by withdrawal of same amount
- Round-dollar wire transfers (e.g., exactly $50,000.00)
- Wire transfers to/from unrelated third parties

#### **Crypto-Specific Red Flags**
- Deposits from cryptocurrency mixer/tumbler (Tornado Cash, Blender.io)
- Rapid conversion of crypto to fiat and immediate withdrawal
- Use of privacy coins (Monero, Zcash) for large transactions
- Deposits from darknet market addresses (identified via blockchain analytics)

#### **Inconsistent Activity**
- Transactions inconsistent with customer's stated occupation or source of funds
- Sudden increase in account activity (10x normal volume)
- Customer unable to explain source of funds when asked

#### **Geographic Red Flags**
- Transactions involving FATF high-risk jurisdictions
- IP address from sanctioned country accessing account
- Customer conducts business in location inconsistent with stated residence

#### **Customer Behavior Red Flags**
- Customer reluctant to provide CIP/CDD information
- Customer provides false or misleading information
- Customer inquires about AML/SAR reporting requirements
- Customer appears to be acting on behalf of undisclosed third party

## 7. Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) Filing

### A. Legal Requirement

**31 CFR Â§ 1023.320** requires financial institutions to file a **Suspicious Activity Report (SAR)** with FinCEN for any transaction or pattern of transactions of **$5,000 or more** where:

1. The transaction involves funds derived from illegal activities
2. The transaction is designed to evade BSA regulations (structuring)
3. The transaction has no business or lawful purpose
4. The transaction involves terrorist financing

### B. SAR Filing Thresholds

| Scenario | Amount Threshold | Filing Deadline |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Insider abuse** (employee theft, fraud) | Any amount | 30 days |
| **Transactions involving potential money laundering** | â‰¥$5,000 | 30 days |
| **Structuring** (attempting to evade CTR) | Any amount | 30 days |
| **Computer intrusion** (hacking, data breach affecting accounts) | Any amount | 30 days |
| **Terrorist financing** (any suspicion of terrorism) | Any amount | **Immediately** |

**Filing Deadline:**
- File SAR within **30 calendar days** of initial detection of suspicious activity
- If unable to identify suspect, may extend to **60 calendar days**
- Terrorist financing SARs: File immediately, contact FBI

### C. SAR Filing Form

**FinCEN Form 111** - SAR-SF (Suspicious Activity Report - Securities and Futures)

**Required Information:**
- Financial institution information (BlackRoad OS, Inc., EIN, address)
- Suspect information (name, DOB, SSN, address, occupation)
- Suspicious activity details (dates, amounts, accounts involved)
- **Narrative:** Detailed description of suspicious activity (who, what, when, where, why suspicious)

**Narrative Best Practices:**
- Minimum 200 words, comprehensive description
- Include specific dates, amounts, transaction types
- Explain WHY activity is suspicious (not just what occurred)
- Avoid conclusions ("customer is laundering money") - state facts only
- Include customer's explanation (if provided)

### D. SAR Confidentiality - CRITICAL

**18 U.S.C. Â§ 1517:** "Tipping off" a customer about a SAR filing is a **FEDERAL CRIME**.

**PROHIBITED:**
- âŒ Notifying customer that SAR has been filed
- âŒ Disclosing existence of SAR to customer
- âŒ Revealing that transaction is being investigated
- âŒ Providing copy of SAR to customer or their attorney

**Penalty for Tipping Off:**
- **Civil:** Unlimited liability for damages
- **Criminal:** Up to **5 years imprisonment** per violation

**EXCEPTION:** SARs may be disclosed to:
- FinCEN and other government agencies
- Federal/state law enforcement upon request
- Federal regulatory agencies (SEC, FINRA)
- Self-regulatory organizations (FINRA) upon request
- **NOT** to customer, even if subpoenaed by customer's attorney

### E. SAR Filing Process

**Step 1: AMLCO Determination**
- AMLCO completes investigation (see Section 6)
- Determines SAR filing is required based on criteria

**Step 2: Prepare SAR**
- Complete FinCEN Form 111 via BSA E-Filing System
- Draft comprehensive narrative (minimum 200 words)
- Attach supporting documentation (not sent to FinCEN, maintained internally)

**Step 3: File SAR Electronically**
- Submit via FinCEN BSA E-Filing System: https://bsaefiling.fincen.treas.gov
- Obtain acknowledgement number
- Save PDF copy of filed SAR

**Step 4: Notify Senior Management**
- Inform CEO/Board that SAR has been filed (do not provide details if they are subject)
- Document notification in compliance log

**Step 5: Continuous Monitoring**
- Continue monitoring account (do not close account immediately after SAR)
- Closing account immediately after suspicious activity may tip off customer
- If account must be closed, wait minimum 30-60 days and provide generic reason

**Step 6: Recordkeeping**
- Maintain copy of SAR for **5 years** from date of filing
- Maintain supporting documentation (investigation notes, transaction records)
- Track in SAR log

## 8. Currency Transaction Report (CTR) Filing

### A. Legal Requirement

**31 CFR Â§ 1010.311** requires financial institutions to file a **Currency Transaction Report (CTR)** for any **cash transaction** exceeding **$10,000** in a single day.

### B. CTR Filing Thresholds

**File CTR for:**
- Cash deposit of **$10,000 or more**
- Cash withdrawal of **$10,000 or more**
- **Aggregated cash transactions** totaling **$10,000 or more** in a single business day by/for the same customer

**Examples:**
- Customer deposits $6,000 cash in morning, $5,000 cash in afternoon â†’ **CTR REQUIRED** (total $11,000)
- Customer withdraws $9,500 cash â†’ No CTR required (but monitor for structuring)
- Customer deposits $10,000 check â†’ **No CTR** (check is not cash/currency)

**Definition of "Currency":**
- âœ… U.S. paper bills and coins
- âœ… Foreign currency (euros, pesos, yen)
- âŒ Checks, money orders, cashier's checks (these are monetary instruments, not currency)
- âŒ Wire transfers (not cash)

### C. CTR Filing Form

**FinCEN Form 112** - Currency Transaction Report

**Required Information:**
- Customer information (name, DOB, SSN, address, occupation, employer)
- Transaction details (date, amount, type - deposit/withdrawal)
- Account information (account number, account type)
- **Person conducting transaction** (if different from customer)

### D. CTR Filing Deadline

File CTR within **15 calendar days** of the cash transaction.

**Filing Method:**
- Electronic filing via FinCEN BSA E-Filing System: https://bsaefiling.fincen.treas.gov

### E. Multiple Transactions (Aggregation Rule)

If customer conducts multiple cash transactions in a single business day totaling **$10,000 or more**, the Firm aggregates them and files a **single CTR** for the total amount.

**Example:**
- 9:00 AM: $4,000 cash deposit
- 11:00 AM: $3,500 cash deposit
- 2:00 PM: $3,000 cash deposit
- **Total: $10,500** â†’ File CTR for $10,500

### F. Structuring (Illegal Evasion of CTR Requirement)

**31 U.S.C. Â§ 5324** makes it a federal crime to structure transactions to evade CTR reporting.

**Red Flags for Structuring:**
- Multiple cash transactions just below $10,000 (e.g., $9,000, $9,500, $9,800)
- Customer specifically requests to keep transaction below $10,000
- Customer asks questions about CTR reporting threshold
- Customer splits cash deposit between multiple accounts

**If Structuring Suspected:**
1. âœ… **File CTR anyway** (even if individual transactions below $10,000)
2. âœ… **File SAR** for structuring (see Section 7)
3. âœ… **Continue monitoring** account for additional structuring
4. âœ… **Do NOT warn customer** that structuring is illegal (tipping off)

### G. CTR Recordkeeping

- U.S. financial institutions are **PROHIBITED** from processing wires to/from shell banks
- Shell bank: Bank with no physical presence in any country

### C. Cash-Intensive Businesses

Customers operating cash-intensive businesses are higher risk for money laundering:

**Examples:**
- Check cashing services
- Money service businesses (MSBs)
- Casinos and gambling
- Restaurants and bars
- Car washes, laundromats
- Retail stores (convenience stores, gas stations)

**Enhanced Procedures:**
- Require **business license** and documentation of business operations
- Verify business location (Google Street View, site visit)
- Request **tax returns** for business
- Conduct **enhanced transaction monitoring** (review 100% of cash deposits)
- Quarterly CDD updates

## 14. Special Considerations for Crypto Assets

### A. AML Risks for Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency presents unique AML risks:

- **Pseudonymity:** Wallet addresses not directly linked to customer identity
- **Mixers/Tumblers:** Services that obscure source of funds (Tornado Cash, Blender.io)
- **Privacy Coins:** Cryptocurrencies designed for anonymity (Monero, Zcash)
- **Decentralized Exchanges (DEXs):** No KYC/AML controls
- **Cross-Border:** Instant global transfers without intermediaries
- **Darknet Markets:** Illicit marketplaces using crypto

### B. Blockchain Analytics

The Firm uses blockchain analytics tools to monitor crypto transactions:

**Recommended Providers:**
- Chainalysis (AML compliance, sanctions screening)
- Elliptic (transaction monitoring, wallet screening)
- CipherTrace (risk scoring, exchange screening)

**Use Cases:**
- Screen incoming crypto deposits for links to mixers, darknet markets, sanctioned addresses
- Trace source of funds for large crypto deposits (>$10K)
- Monitor customer crypto withdrawals for suspicious destinations
- Generate risk scores for crypto addresses

### C. Red Flags for Crypto AML

**High-Risk Crypto Activity:**
- âœ… Deposit from **mixer/tumbler** (Tornado Cash, Blender.io, ChipMixer)
- âœ… Deposit from **darknet market** address (Silk Road, AlphaBay)
- âœ… Use of **privacy coins** (Monero, Zcash) for large transactions
- âœ… **Rapid conversion** of crypto to fiat + immediate withdrawal
- âœ… **Layering:** Multiple crypto-to-crypto conversions to obscure source
- âœ… Deposit from **OFAC-sanctioned address** (North Korean, ransomware wallets)

**If Red Flag Detected:**
1. âœ… **Freeze account** (prevent withdrawals)
2. âœ… **Conduct investigation** (blockchain tracing)
3. âœ… **Request customer explanation**
4. âœ… **File SAR if appropriate**
5. âœ… **OFAC screening** if sanctioned address suspected

If the Firm uses a **qualified custodian** for crypto custody (Coinbase Custody, Fidelity Digital Assets, Anchorage, BitGo Trust), the Firm may **rely** on the custodian's AML/KYC procedures for:

**Requirements for Reliance:**
