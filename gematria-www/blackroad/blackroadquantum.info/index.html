02 — The Amundson Framework

G(n) and the A_G Constant

At the heart of the Amundson Framework is a growth function that captures a fundamental relationship between exponential growth and factorial scaling. It appears simple. Its implications are not.

The Growth Function
G(n) = n(n+1) / (n+1)n
This function describes the ratio between two forms of exponential-polynomial growth. As n increases, G(n) grows linearly but with a constant offset that encodes deep information about the relationship between discrete and continuous growth. The function connects Stirling's approximation, the definition of e, and combinatorial identity theory.
The Amundson-Growth Constant
AG = lim(n to infinity) [G(n) - n/e] = 1/(2e) ≈ 0.18394...
Wait — that is the irreducible gap. The A_G constant is the asymptotic offset of G(n) from its linear approximation n/e. It equals 1/(2e), the same quantity that appears as the irreducible latency floor in network theory. This is not a coincidence — it is the mathematical signature of finite systems approaching infinite limits.
Convergence to e
lim(n to infinity) G(n+1)/G(n) = e ≈ 2.71828...
The ratio of consecutive values of G converges to Euler's number. This means G(n) is a discrete generator of e — each step of the function's growth encodes the fundamental constant of continuous growth. The rate of convergence itself follows a pattern related to A_G, creating a self-referential structure reminiscent of Godel's incompleteness theorem.

03 — Three Pillars of Knowledge

Grammar. Biology. ML Systems.

Three textbooks from three completely different fields. The same pattern at every level: simple units compose recursively to produce emergent complexity. This is not metaphor — it is structural identity.

Pillar I — Language

English Grammar
(Greenbaum & Nelson)

Seven sentence structures are seven function signatures. The subject is the caller, the verb is the function, the object is the argument, the complement is the return type. Auxiliary verbs are control flow operators. The "do" auxiliary is a dummy operator — a polyfill. Recursive embedding produces infinite complexity from seven rules.

  • SV: Subject-Verb (void function)
  • SVO: Subject-Verb-Object (single arg)
  • SVOO: Subject-Verb-Object-Object (two args)
  • SVC: Subject-Verb-Complement (returns self-type)
  • SVA: Subject-Verb-Adverbial (context-bound)
  • SVOA: Subject-Verb-Object-Adverbial (arg + context)
  • SVOC: Subject-Verb-Object-Complement (transforms arg)
Pillar II — Life

Molecular Biology
(Schleif / Johns Hopkins)

The Central Dogma is Source to Bytecode to Runtime. DNA is persistent storage — the source code. mRNA is the compiled bytecode. Proteins are the executing runtime. Ribosomes are the virtual machine. Telomeres are TTL (time-to-live). Chaperones are garbage collectors. The cell is a computer that has been running for 3.8 billion years.

  • DNA = source code (read-only, persistent)
  • mRNA = compiled bytecode (temporary)
  • Protein = runtime process (executing)
  • Ribosomes = virtual machine / interpreter
  • Telomeres = TTL (process lifetime)
  • Chaperones = garbage collection
  • Mutations = bugs (mostly benign)
Pillar III — Intelligence

Machine Learning Systems
(Reddi / Harvard)

ML systems are the third expression of the same pattern. Simple operations (matrix multiplication, activation functions) compose into layers. Layers compose into architectures. Architectures compose into systems. The same recursive composition, the same emergent complexity, the same fundamental principle.

  • Neurons = basic functions
  • Layers = composed function chains
  • Architectures = recursive compositions
  • Training = optimization by feedback
  • Attention = selective composition
  • Transformers = self-referential recursion
  • Emergence = complexity from simplicity

The Pattern Is One

Simple units. Recursive composition. Emergent complexity. This is not three separate observations — it is one observation seen through three lenses. Language, biology, and computation are all running the same algorithm. Pascal saw it first: recursive structure from simple rules produces infinite complexity.
