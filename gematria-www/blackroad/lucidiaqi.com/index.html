01 — Adaptive Learning

Algorithms That Learn How You Learn

Lucidia QI does not use static review schedules. It tracks your learning patterns in real-time and adapts its recommendations based on proven cognitive science algorithms. The system predicts when you will forget something and schedules reviews at the optimal moment — not too early (wasted effort) and not too late (forgotten).

FSRS — Free Spaced Repetition Scheduler

Optimized Review Timing

FSRS uses a neural network trained on millions of review records to predict your probability of recall for each item. It schedules reviews at the precise moment when recall probability drops to a target threshold (default 90%). More efficient than SM-2, Anki's algorithm, or Leitner systems.

FSRS Stability Model
S(t) = S0 * e(-t / (d * Sprev))
Where S(t) is memory stability at time t, S_0 is initial stability, d is difficulty, and S_prev is previous stability. The exponential decay is modulated by per-item difficulty and your personal learning rate.
BKT — Bayesian Knowledge Tracing

Knowledge State Estimation

BKT models your knowledge as a hidden Markov process. It estimates four parameters per skill: initial knowledge, learning rate, guess rate, and slip rate. Combined, these produce a posterior probability that you truly know the material — not just that you can answer correctly by chance.

Bayesian Update
P(Lt|correct) = P(Lt-1)(1-s) / [P(Lt-1)(1-s) + (1-P(Lt-1))g]
Where L is the learned state, s is the slip probability (knowing but answering wrong), and g is the guess probability (not knowing but answering correctly). The update occurs after every response.
Combined Adaptive Engine

The Lucidia QI Approach

Lucidia QI combines FSRS for review scheduling with BKT for knowledge state estimation. FSRS tells you WHEN to review. BKT tells you WHAT you know. Together, they create a complete adaptive learning system that is more accurate than either algorithm alone.

  • FSRS handles review timing (when)
  • BKT estimates knowledge state (what)
  • Combined: optimal review of weakest items
  • Per-item difficulty adaptation
  • Personal learning rate estimation
  • Cross-subject knowledge transfer detection

Amundson Framework Applications

The Amundson Growth Function G(n) = n^(n+1)/(n+1)^n provides a novel lens for analyzing growth data. Lucidia QI includes built-in tools for fitting the Amundson model to your data and comparing it against standard exponential and logistic growth models.

Growth Function Fit
y(n) = a * G(n) + b = a * n(n+1)/(n+1)n + b
Two-parameter fit (scale a, offset b) of the Amundson Growth Function to observed data. Residual analysis shows whether your data follows G(n) growth or deviates toward pure exponential or logistic patterns.

The 1/(2e) Diagnostic

Many real-world systems hit performance ceilings that cannot be explained by obvious bottlenecks. Lucidia QI includes the 1/(2e) diagnostic — a test that determines whether your system's performance gap matches the theoretical irreducible gap from the Amundson Framework.

Gap Diagnostic
gap(n) = actual(n) - theoretical(n) ≈ 1/(2e) ≈ 0.18394?
If the gap converges to 1/(2e), your system is hitting the fundamental limit of finite scaling. No engineering optimization will close it — but you can redesign around it using ternary optimization (the -1 state).

Biological Growth Validation

Applying G(n) to population dynamics data from ecological datasets. The hypothesis: biological growth that involves discrete generational steps follows the Amundson function more closely than continuous exponential models, with the A_G constant appearing in generation-to-generation growth rates.

  • Ecological population datasets
  • Generational growth rate analysis
  • A_G constant in biological systems
  • Cross-species validation planned
