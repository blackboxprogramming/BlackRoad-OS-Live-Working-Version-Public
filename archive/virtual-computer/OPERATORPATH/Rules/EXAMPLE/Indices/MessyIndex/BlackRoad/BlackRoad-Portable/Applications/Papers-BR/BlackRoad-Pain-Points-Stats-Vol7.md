## 3. THE ALGORITHMIC RATINGS TRAP
**Relevant to:** RoadWork, RoadRound, RoadWorld
*Your livelihood is controlled by a star rating from a stranger who might just be having a bad day.*

| Stat | Source |
|------|--------|
| 36% of the U.S. workforce (57 million people) now participates in gig work | UN SDGs / Protecting Worker Earnings 2024 |
| Gig workers earn as little as $5.12/hour after expenses — well below federal minimum wage | Human Rights Watch "The Gig Trap" 2025 |
| Workers of color receive average ratings of 4.72 vs. 4.79 for white workers — a gap that triggers deactivation thresholds | Harvard Business Review / Yale SOM 2024–25 |
| Nearly half of gig worker account deactivations were later found to be erroneous and reinstated | HRW "The Gig Trap" 2025 |
| Platforms including Uber, DoorDash, and Amazon Flex operate fully opaque deactivation algorithms | HRW 2025 |
| Once a low rating is displayed publicly, non-biased customers still avoid low-rated workers — bias compounds itself | Yale SOM 2025 |
| Workers have no meaningful avenue to challenge ratings or algorithmic decisions on most platforms | Zero Hour Lab / HRW 2025 |
| Rating-linked wage suppression disproportionately impacts racial minorities and marginalized workers | HBR 2024–25 |
| 70,000-rating analysis found racial bias in scores with no corresponding quality difference | Yale SOM 2024 |
| Unpredictable income, high stress, and zero recourse define 36% of U.S. workers' daily experience | UN SDGs 2024 |

**The story in one line:** A 1-star rating from someone who doesn't look like you can end your income. There's no appeal. No human. No record.
