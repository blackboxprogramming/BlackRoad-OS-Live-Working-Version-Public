# RoadWork — "The Tax"
**Type**: Long-form Brand Ad — Full DI/Prose Interpretation
**Date**: April 10, 2026
**Product**: RoadWork (roadwork.blackroad.io)
**Style**: NSDA Dramatic/Prose Interpretation — multiple voices, full emotional arc
**Theme**: The administration is the tax on the dream. RoadWork pays it for you.
**Core emotion**: The night you get back to the thing you actually started it for.

---

## [INTRODUCTION — narrator, close, like a confession]

You didn't start it for this.

Whatever it is you built —

the shop, the practice, the agency, the studio, the thing you quit your job for —

you didn't start it for this.

You started it for the thing.

The actual thing.

The reason you couldn't not do it.

And somewhere along the way —

quietly,

without anyone announcing it —

the thing became the smallest part of your day.

---

## [SCENE ONE — narrator, portrait, specific]

Her name is Jamie.

She is thirty-seven years old.

She is a photographer.

She is not taking photographs right now.

It is 11:42pm.

She is writing an invoice.

---

Not her first tonight.

She wrote two before dinner.

She sent a follow-up email on the one from March —

the client who said *"I'll get to it"*

for the fourth time —

she sent that at 7pm.

She rescheduled a shoot at 8.

She responded to a contract question at 9.

She updated her books at 10.

She started the invoice at 11.

She is finishing it now.

At 11:42.

On a Wednesday.

---

On her desk there is a camera.

She hasn't touched it today.

---

## [PAUSE.]

---

## [NARRATOR — lower]

There is a list in Jamie's head.

It never gets shorter.

It just changes shape.

*Invoice the Hendersons.*

*Follow up with Marcus — three weeks overdue.*

*Rebook the Saturday session.*

*Update the contract template.*

*Respond to the inquiry from last Tuesday.*

*File the quarterly.*

*Schedule the vendor call.*

*Update the portfolio site.*

*Write the proposal.*

*Chase the proposal you sent.*

*Write the next one.*

---

And underneath all of it —

underneath every line of that list —

the one thing that never makes it onto the list

because it doesn't feel like work:

*take photographs.*

*The thing she built this for.*

*The thing she is not doing.*

---

## [VOICE SHIFT — the list. Flat. Relentless. No malice. Just endless.]

*Invoice. Follow up. Rebook. Update. Respond. File. Schedule. Chase. Repeat.*

---

## [NARRATOR. Tired. Honest.]

She doesn't tell people this part.

They see the Instagram.

They see the beautiful work.

They say —

*"It must be so amazing to do what you love."*

And she smiles.

Because she does love it.

She loves it so much she built a whole life around it.

She loves it so much that this —

*this* —

all of this grinding administrative weight —

is worth it.

Or it was.

---

There are nights she's not sure it still is.

There are nights she sits at this desk at midnight

writing invoices

and she tries to remember the last time she felt it —

the feeling she started for —

the specific stillness of a good frame

the way light moves through a room

the exact moment before —

and she can't quite reach it.

It's there.

It's there.

But it's under the list.

---

## [LONG PAUSE.]

---

## [SCENE TWO — narrator, the turn]

Someone tells Jamie about RoadWork.

She is skeptical.

She has tried things.

Things that promised to simplify

and added three new things to learn.

But she tries it.

---

She tells it about the Hendersons.

She tells it about Marcus.

She tells it about the contract template that needs updating

and the quarterly that's almost due

and the Saturday rebook

and the unanswered inquiry.

---

And RoadWork says —

---

## [VOICE SHIFT — RoadWork. Calm. Capable. Not excited. Just ready.]

*"Got it. I'll invoice the Hendersons tonight and follow up with Marcus in the morning — the fourth follow-up, so I'll adjust the tone. The contract template — I found three clauses that need updating based on what you described. I'll draft it. The quarterly is due in eleven days. I'll have it ready in three. The Saturday rebook — I sent them three options, they just confirmed the 14th. The inquiry from Tuesday — I replied with your pricing and availability. They wrote back. They want to book."*

---

## [NARRATOR. Still. The weight lifting in real time.]

Jamie reads it.

She reads it again.

*They want to book.*

*The Saturday is rebooked.*

*Marcus is being followed up with.*

*The invoice is sent.*

---

She looks at the time.

It is 9:17pm.

---

She picks up her camera.

She walks to the window.

The light is doing something strange with the curtain.

She shoots it.

Then she shoots it again.

Then she moves the lamp.

She shoots it again.

She doesn't stop until 11.

---

## [SCENE THREE — quiet, final]

The next morning she sells one of those shots.

Not because it was planned.

Because she had the time to take it.

Because for the first time in a year

the hours after dinner

belonged to the thing she built this for.

Not the list.

*The thing.*

---

## [NARRATOR — building to the final turn]

Here is the truth about running a business alone.

Nobody warns you about the administration.

They warn you about the risk.

The market. The competition. The funding.

Nobody says:

*"You will spend forty percent of your time on tasks that have nothing to do with why you started."*

Nobody says:

*"The thing you love will slowly become the smallest part of your day."*

Nobody says:

*"The tax on the dream is this — all of this — and you will pay it alone."*

---

But here is what I want you to hear.

You are not supposed to do all of it.

You were never supposed to do all of it.

You built something real.

You have real work to do.

The invoices, the follow-ups, the scheduling, the contracts, the filings —

that is not your work.

That is the tax on your work.

And taxes

can be handled.

---

## [OUTRO. Slow. Direct.]

You didn't start it to invoice.

You didn't start it to follow up.

You didn't start it to schedule and rebook and file and chase.

You started it for the thing.

*Go do the thing.*

---

RoadWork.

The business runs.

You build.
