# RoadWorld — "Fifty Million"
*DI/Prose Interpretation style — Brand Ad*
*60–90 seconds VO | Emotional register: wonder → cold math → sovereignty*

---

## SCRIPT

---

He was fourteen when he built it.

A world.

Mountains. A river system. A cave network that took three weeks.
A town with working shops and a jail and a bell that rang on the hour.

He built it because he couldn't stop.
Because it was *there* when he closed his eyes
and he just had to make it real.

---

Fifty million visits.

Fifty million people walked through the door he made.
Climbed the mountains.
Found the cave.
Heard the bell.

---

He was seventeen by then.

---

The platform took seventy percent.

Of the in-world currency.
Which you then had to convert.
At their rate.
On their schedule.
If you met their payout threshold.
Which changed.

---

He did the math once.

Fifty million visits.

He made enough to buy a used car.

Not a good one.

---

*The world was his.*

*The economy was theirs.*

---

That's not a bug.
That's the architecture.

You build inside their walls —
your creativity, your hours, your vision —

and the monetization layer belongs to the platform.

Always has.

They call it a marketplace.

It's a landlord.

---

RoadWorld is different.

Build your world.
On your infrastructure.
Under your domain.

Your economy — your currency, your rules, your conversion rate.
Your community — your data, your members, your moderation.
Your IP — the mountains, the river, the bell.

*Yours.*

---

Not licensed back to you.
Not subject to a policy update that changes the payout floor.
Not at risk because the platform pivoted to something else
and your world is now three menu layers deep.

---

Fifty million people found what he made.

The next fifty million will find it

where he owns the door.

---

**RoadWorld.**
*Build it somewhere it's actually yours.*
