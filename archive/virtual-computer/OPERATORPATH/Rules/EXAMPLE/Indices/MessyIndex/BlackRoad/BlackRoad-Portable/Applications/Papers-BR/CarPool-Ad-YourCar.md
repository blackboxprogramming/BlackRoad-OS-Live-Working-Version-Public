# CarPool — "Your Car"
*DI/Prose Interpretation style — Brand Ad*
*60–90 seconds VO | Emotional register: quiet dignity → the math → refusal*

---

## SCRIPT

---

Your car.

Your insurance.

Your gas.

Your phone mount, your aux cord, your phone charger in the back
because you know someone always needs it.

Your Febreze.
Your phone plan — unlimited, because the app runs all day.
Your time.
Your knees.
Your lower back.

Yours.

---

They provide the app.

---

For that,

they take forty percent.

---

Not forty percent of profit.
Forty percent of the fare.
Before gas.
Before the car payment.
Before the wear on the tires that are,
as previously established,

yours.

---

There's a man in Atlanta.
He drives six days a week.
He did the math in February — really did it.
Wrote it down.

After gas, after the car payment, after insurance,
after the $14 he spends on water bottles because five-star reviews
are the only job security he has —

He nets $9.40 an hour.

---

The app made $8.4 billion last year.

---

He knows that number.

He looked it up in the parking lot
while waiting for the next ping.

---

*That's not a gig.*

*That's a job with no floor, no ceiling, no HR department,*
*and a rating system that can end your income*
*before you finish reading the one-star review.*

---

CarPool is different.

Peer to peer.
Driver sets the rate.
Passenger pays the rate.
Platform takes a flat fee —
not a percentage,
not a cut that scales with your hustle.

A flat fee.

---

The rest is yours.

Your car.
Your rate.
Your income.

An algorithm that works *for* you,
not one that decides
what your time is worth.

---

The man in Atlanta did the math again.

Different numbers this time.

---

He drove five days instead of six.

Made more.

---

**CarPool.**
*Drive on your terms.*
