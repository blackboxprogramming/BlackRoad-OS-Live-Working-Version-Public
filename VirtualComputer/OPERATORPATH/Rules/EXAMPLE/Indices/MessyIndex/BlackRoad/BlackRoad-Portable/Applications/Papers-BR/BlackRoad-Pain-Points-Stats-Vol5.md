## GIG WORKER BENEFITS VOID
*(CarPool · RoadWork · RoadPay)*

- An estimated **36–57 million Americans** participate in gig or contingent work
- Only **12.8%** of gig/temp workers receive employer-sponsored health insurance
  — compare to **53%+** of traditional employees
- Only **6.6%** of gig workers have access to any employer-backed retirement plan
  — compare to **46.3%** of traditional employees
- **27%** of gig workers who rely on gig work as their main income have **zero** retirement savings
- **28%** of gig workers wish they had health insurance — they know what they're missing
- No sick leave. No unemployment insurance. No paid time off.
- Income volatility is the norm: gig workers consistently score lower on financial well-being
- The flexibility is real. So is the freefall.

## STUDENT DEBT TRAP
*(BlackBoard · Roadie · RoadWork)*

- Total U.S. student loan debt: **$1.8 trillion** — second-largest consumer debt category after mortgages
- **45+ million Americans** carry student loan debt — about **1 in 6 adults**
- Average federal student loan debt per borrower: **$39,547–$39,633** (2025 record)
- Medical/law graduates: **$140,000–$200,000** in debt
- **66%** of all student loan debt is held by **women**
- **Black borrowers** are significantly more likely to take out loans (66%) than white borrowers (47%)
- After pandemic payment pauses ended: **~20–24%** of borrowers fell behind on payments
- **9 million+ borrowers** missed at least one payment in 2025
- **42%** of federal loan borrowers struggle to cover rent, food, and utilities due to loan payments
- The Pell Grant now covers only **31%** of expenses at public universities
- Debt grew by **$60 billion** from 2024 to 2025 — the crisis is still accelerating

## HOUSING CRISIS: COST BURDEN
*(BackRoad · CarPool · RoadWork)*

- **22.7 million renter households** (49–52% of all renters) were cost-burdened in 2024
  — meaning they spent 30%+ of income on housing
- **11.2 million renter households** spent more than **50% of income** on rent — a record
- **33% of ALL U.S. households** (renters + owners) are cost-burdened — 43.5 million homes
- Renters' median housing costs rose **38%** from 2019–2024; incomes rose only **28%**
- Rents jumped **26%** from 2020–2024; home prices **47%**
- **56% of Black renters** and **54% of Hispanic renters** are cost-burdened
  vs. **47% of white renters**
- U.S. shortage of affordable units: over **7 million homes** for extremely low-income renters
- **69% of Americans** are "very concerned" about the cost of housing (Pew 2024)
- There is no app that fixes this. But an economy that returns income to workers helps.

## AI JOB DISPLACEMENT
*(RoadTrip · RoadCode · OfficeRoad · BlackRoad OS)*

- WEF projects AI will displace **85 million jobs** globally by 2025 — and create 97 million new ones
  (Net positive on paper. The gap in between is where real people live.)
- **14%** of workers globally have already experienced AI-related job displacement (2024)
- **~30%** of current U.S. jobs could be automated by 2030
- **60%** of all U.S. jobs will undergo significant task-level change due to AI integration
- **2.1 million American workers** expected to lose jobs to AI automation in 2025 alone
- **66% of enterprises** are already reducing entry-level hiring due to AI
- **91% of companies** report roles have already been changed or eliminated by automation
- Customer service: **80%** automation potential. Data entry: **65%**.
- New AI roles require a **master's degree** in 77% of cases — a steep re-entry bar
- AI-skilled workers earn **56% more** than those without AI skills (PwC 2025)
- **60% of jobs** in advanced countries face meaningful AI exposure (IMF) vs. 26% in low-income countries
- Women's jobs are more exposed to AI automation than men's
- The jobs being automated away first are the entry-level ones that used to be the ladder

## MUSIC STREAMING INEQUALITY
*(RoadCoin · BackRoad · OneWay)*

- Spotify paid a record **$10 billion** in royalties in 2024 — over 10x more than a decade ago
- But: **12 million artists** uploaded music to Spotify last year
- Less than **0.6%** of uploaders earned $10,000 or more
- Less than **2.3%** earned $1,000 or more
- The average royalty rate: **$3.00 per 1,000 streams** (Apple Music pays $8.80; Amazon $6.20)
- As of 2024: tracks must hit **1,000 annual streams** before any royalties are paid at all
- **1,450 artists** earned $1 million+ — out of 12 million uploaders
- Independent labels and artists earned ~**$5 billion** of Spotify's $10B payout — roughly half
- The model: Spotify compares it to professional sports. Few earn a living. Most play for free.
- 12 million artists. 1,450 millionaires. 99.4% made less than $10K.

## CREATOR ECONOMY INCOME INEQUALITY
*(BackRoad · OneWay · RoadView · RoadCoin)*

- The global creator economy: **$205.25 billion** (2024)
- Only **4%** of creators earn more than $100,000/year
- **73%** of creators make less than **$30,000/year**
- **26%** of influencers make less than **$1,000/year**
- Median annual creator income: **$3,000** — down from $3,500 the year before
- The top 10% of creators capture **62% of all ad revenue** (up from 53% two years ago)
- At the top: MrBeast earns ~$85M/year; Cristiano Ronaldo earns $3.23M **per Instagram post**
- The gender gap: male creators earn nearly **double** what female creators earn ($69,922 vs $37,065)
  — despite women making up **70%** of the influencer market
- Top earners have **7+ revenue streams**. The bottom 73% average **two**.
- The creator economy looks like a gold rush from the outside.
  From inside: it's a lottery where 73% of tickets pay less than minimum wage.

## APP STORE TAX
*(RoadCode · RoadCoin · RoadWork)*

- Apple's standard App Store fee: **30%** of all digital transactions
  (15% for developers under $1M/year via "Small Business Program")
- Apple made over **$10 billion** in U.S. App Store commissions in 2024 alone
- Globally: Apple collected **$27+ billion** in App Store fees in 2024
- App Store + Google Play combined: **$127 billion** in consumer spending in 2024
- Google Play standard cut: **30%** (drops to 15% after first $1M; 10% for some categories)
- A 2024 court ruling forced Apple to allow links to third-party payment methods
  — Apple immediately charged a new **27% commission** on those links instead
- Nearly **half of all app store revenue** now comes from subscriptions
- The EU and U.S. are pushing back — but enforcement is slow
- If you build on their platform, they take 15–30% of everything you earn. Forever.

## NONPROFIT FUNDRAISING TAX
*(OfficeRoad · RoadWork)*

- Standard payment processing fees for nonprofits: **2.2%–2.9% + $0.30 per transaction**
- Platform/subscription fees on top: **2–5% per gift** or **$0–$200+/month**
- Payout/withdrawal fees: **up to 3%** for expedited transfers
- "Free" platforms use **donor tipping models** — defaulted at 15–20%, often misunderstood
- Average nonprofit overhead (including tech + admin + fundraising): **~27%**
- Best-in-class nonprofits keep overhead closer to **18%**
- **7 in 10 nonprofits** report needing more revenue streams than 2 years ago
- **62–66%** are seeking new solutions for donor tracking and online fundraising management
- Automation is rising — but every new tool adds another fee layer
- Every dollar donated passes through multiple hands before it reaches the mission.
  The platforms take their cut first.
