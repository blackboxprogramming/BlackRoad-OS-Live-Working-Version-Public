# RoadChat — "What You Told It"
*DI/Prose Interpretation style — Brand Ad*
*60–90 seconds VO | Emotional register: intimate → unsettled → sovereign*

---

## SCRIPT

---

You told it about the business idea at 1am.

The one you hadn't told anyone yet.
Not your partner. Not your co-founder.
Not the friend who would have been honest.

You told the AI.

---

You told it about the diagnosis.
You were processing. You needed to think out loud.
You weren't ready to call anyone.

You told the AI.

---

You told it the thing you're afraid of.
The strategy you're not sure will work.
The product you're building in secret.
The pitch you're rehearsing for the round.

You told it everything.

---

*And every word of it*

*belongs to them.*

---

Not maliciously.
It's in the terms.
Paragraph four, section two, subsection C.

*"Interactions may be used to improve model performance."*

---

That's not a threat.
That's a business model.

Your most private thinking —
your 1am ideas,
your unfinished sentences,
your fear dressed up as a question —

is training data.

---

For them.

---

There's a man in Seattle.
He runs a small biotech.
He used a major AI assistant to draft a patent claim.
He described the mechanism.
The novel part.
The thing no one else had figured out yet.

He didn't think about where that went.

---

He thinks about it now.

---

RoadChat runs on your infrastructure.

Your keys. Your storage. Your context.

The conversation lives where you put it —
on your machine,
in your org,
under your terms.

---

It remembers what you told it
because *you* own the memory.

Not a server farm in Virginia.
Not a model that's watching what makes you hesitate.

---

*You.*

---

The 1am idea?

Still yours.

The diagnosis?

Still private.

The patent?

Still novel.

---

**RoadChat.**
*Yours to think in. Yours to keep.*
