## DARK PATTERNS & SUBSCRIPTION TRAPS
*(BackRoad · RoadBook · BlackRoad OS)*

- **76%** of 642 subscription-based websites and apps studied use at least one dark pattern (FTC/ICPEN 2024)
- **67%** use more than one
- **81%** use auto-renewal "sneaking" — making it nearly impossible to turn off recurring billing
- **70%** do not provide clear cancellation information
- **90%** of consumers across 20 countries report being affected by dark patterns (OECD)
- **56%** of users lost trust in a platform after encountering manipulative tactics
- Mild dark patterns: **double** the likelihood of unwanted subscriptions
- Aggressive dark patterns: **quadruple** the likelihood
- **40%+** of consumers have experienced negative financial outcomes from deceptive UX
- **67%** of users had difficulty removing payment methods or relinking accounts
- The button to cancel is always smaller. The button to subscribe is always green.

## WAGE THEFT
*(RoadWork · CarPool · CarKeys)*

- Wage theft costs U.S. workers an estimated **$50 billion per year** — all forms combined
- Minimum wage violations alone: **$15 billion/year** stolen (Economic Policy Institute)
- **4 million workers** are paid illegally below minimum wage annually — average loss: **$3,000 each**
- Wage theft total **exceeds all U.S. robberies by 100x** — the most common theft in America nobody talks about
- Only **$1.5 billion** in stolen wages was recovered over all of 2021–2023 — roughly **3%** of what's taken
- The Dept. of Labor has **611 investigators** for **165 million workers** — a 52-year staffing low
- That's a ratio of **1 investigator per 278,000 workers**
- Fortune 500 companies including Walmart, Amazon, FedEx, and Bank of America have all been fined for wage theft
- Common methods: unpaid overtime, off-the-clock work, tip theft, misclassifying employees as contractors
- The crime that outstrips every other theft in America — and barely makes the news

## ELDER FRAUD & DIGITAL EXPLOITATION
*(CarKeys · BackRoad · RoadView)*

- Seniors lost **$4.8 billion** to online scams in 2024 (FBI) — a **33% increase** year-over-year
- FTC reports seniors 60+ lost **$2.4 billion** to scams in 2024 — up **4x** from $600M in 2020
- Actual losses may be **$81.5 billion annually** — most elder fraud is never reported (FTC estimate)
- Average loss per senior victim: **$32,600** — vs. $19,300 for the general population
- Investment scams alone: **$1.2 billion** in elder losses in 2024
- Government impersonation scams: **$789 million** in 2024 — up from $171M the year before
- Tech support scams: **$159 million** lost by seniors in 2024
- Adults 80+: highest median individual loss per case
- Social isolation is a primary risk factor — scammers exploit loneliness as a feature, not a bug
- Technology was supposed to connect seniors. For predators, it opened a direct line.

## FOOD DELIVERY PLATFORM TAX
*(CarPool · RoadWork · OfficeRoad)*

- DoorDash and Uber Eats each processed **$20–21 billion** in U.S. order volume in Q4 2024 alone
- Restaurant commissions: **15–30%** per order, plus payment processing (2.9–4%), marketing (1–5%), and delivery costs
- All-in real cost to restaurants: **35–48% of every order**
- Most restaurants raise app menu prices **10–25%** to compensate
- **48%** of consumers who reduced delivery usage cited inflated menu prices as the reason
- **41%** cited high delivery fees
- Platform fees can make up **20–44%** of the total ticket price consumers pay
- Ticketmaster collected **$16.4 billion in fees** from 2019–2024 (a parallel: both are toll booths on access)
- Delivery app usage grew from **15% → 21%** of consumers from 2020–2024 — dependency deepens as margins shrink
- They don't make the food. They don't deliver the food. They take up to half the money.

## MENTAL HEALTH ACCESS CRISIS
*(Roadie · BackRoad · BlackBoard)*

- **40% of the U.S. population** (137 million people) live in federally designated Mental Health Professional Shortage Areas
- **122 million Americans** lived in shortage areas at the end of 2024
- **6,000+ additional providers** needed just to meet current demand — they don't exist yet
- **62 million U.S. adults** (23%) had a mental illness in 2024
- **Nearly half** (48%) did not receive any treatment
- **56.1%** of young people with major depressive episodes received no care
- **5 million** had serious thoughts of suicide in 2024 — most untreated
- **80%** of people who struggle to access care cite **cost** as the primary barrier
- Digital mental health tools: **$300–$1,500/year** — usually not covered by insurance
- **55% of counselors** say insufficient pay/reimbursement is a major crisis for the field
- The care exists. The system makes it inaccessible.

## EMAIL & COMMUNICATION OVERLOAD
*(OfficeRoad · RoadTrip · BlackRoad OS)*

- Average office worker receives **121–126 emails/day** — double the volume from five years ago
- Workers send **~40 emails/day** and spend **5–15.5 hours per week** on email
- **35%** of employees spend up to **5 hours a day** managing their inbox
- Only **10%** of work emails are business-critical
- That means workers waste on average **10.8 hours per week** on non-critical email
- **44%** of employees report negative feelings about their inbox
- Nearly half say inbox stress **keeps them up at night**
- **60%** report high stress from digital communication overload
- **58%** of remote workers feel pressured to always be reachable
- **376 billion emails** will be sent globally per day by 2025
- The average worker has a full day's worth of work every week that is just… managing email.

## LIVE EVENT TICKET PRICE GOUGING
*(RoadView · BackRoad · OneWay)*

- Average concert ticket price: **$135.92** in 2024 — up from $91.86 in 2019 (+48%)
- Secondary market resale prices: routinely **2x–38x face value**
  — One Hozier ticket (face: $99) listed for **$24,000** on resale
  — Taylor Swift resale average: **$1,000+**, with some reaching $20,000
- Platform fees make up **20–44%** of the total ticket price
- Ticketmaster collected **$16.4 billion in fees** from consumers over 2019–2024 (FTC)
- FTC and states sued Live Nation/Ticketmaster in 2025 for enabling scalpers and deceptive pricing
- Dynamic pricing drove **30%+ price increases** from 2019 to 2024
- Scalper bots now use **AI to evade detection** — real fans compete against algorithms
- Artists don't get the resale markup — it flows to scalpers and the ticketing platform
- **376 billion** global daily emails... and still the only way to find out the show sold out
  is when you see it on StubHub for $800.

## PREDATORY LENDING TRAP
*(RoadWork · CarKeys · BackRoad)*

- Typical payday loan APR: **390%–600%+** in many states
  — Texas: 662% · Mississippi: 572% · Wisconsin: 537% · Alabama: 456%
- Americans took out over **20 million payday/predatory loans** in 2022, totaling **$8.6 billion**
- Lenders collected **$2.4 billion in fees** from these borrowers in one year
- **70–86%** of borrowers take out a new payday loan immediately after paying off the previous one
- Only **20 states** (+ DC) cap rates at 36% APR — the other 30 allow triple-digit exploitation
- Black consumers are **twice as likely** as white consumers to live near a payday lender or pawnshop
- Texas alone: **$1.3 billion** in payday fees extracted from borrowers annually
- The debt trap is documented, systematic, geographic, and racial
- These aren't emergency tools. They are engineered cycles of extraction.
