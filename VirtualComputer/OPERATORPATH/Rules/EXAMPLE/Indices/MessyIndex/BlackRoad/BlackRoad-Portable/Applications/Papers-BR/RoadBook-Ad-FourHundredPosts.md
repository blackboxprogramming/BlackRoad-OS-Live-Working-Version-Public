# RoadBook — "Four Hundred Posts"
*DI/Prose Interpretation style — Brand Ad*
*60–90 seconds VO | Emotional register: grief → defiance → sovereignty*

---

## SCRIPT

---

Four hundred posts.

Seven years.

Every Tuesday morning before the rest of the house was awake —

you wrote.

---

About the thing you know better than anyone.
The hard thing. The real thing.
The thing people email you about because they can't find it explained anywhere else.

---

You built an audience.
Not a big one. Not a famous one.
Eleven thousand people who actually read it.

Who forwarded it.
Who printed it out.
Who said: *this changed how I think.*

---

And then one Tuesday morning,

a different email.

---

*"We're making changes to our platform policy."*

---

Monetization restructured.
Distribution algorithm updated.
Your top posts — the ones that took weeks — 
buried under content the new system preferred.

You watched the numbers fall.
Not because you changed.

Because they did.

---

The work was still there.

But the work was theirs.

The audience you built —
inside their walls,
on their roads,
in their house —

was theirs too.

---

You had been a tenant.

Very productive tenant.

---

There's a woman in London.
She wrote about grief.
Real grief. Her mother. Three years of posts.
Some of the most honest writing on the internet.

The platform changed hands.
New owners. New terms.
She had thirty days to export.

She didn't know until day twenty-nine.

Some of it she got back.

Not all.

---

Your knowledge doesn't belong on their platform.

It belongs in your book.

---

RoadBook is your sovereign publishing layer.

Write it there.
It lives in your domain.
Your export.
Your archive.
Your intellectual property —
not licensed to a platform,
not subject to a policy update,
not held hostage by a terms of service
you agreed to in 2018 and haven't read since.

---

Your audience follows you
because of the work.

Not the platform.

---

Give them a place that's actually yours.

---

Four hundred posts.

Seven years.

Tuesday mornings.

*Yours.*

---

**RoadBook.**
*Write it somewhere you own.*
