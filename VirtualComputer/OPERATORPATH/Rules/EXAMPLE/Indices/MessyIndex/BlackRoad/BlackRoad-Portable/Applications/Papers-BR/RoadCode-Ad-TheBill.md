# RoadCode — "The Bill"
*DI/Prose Interpretation style — Brand Ad*
*60–90 seconds VO | Emotional register: dark comedy → exhaustion → relief*

---

## SCRIPT

---

Before you write a line of code,

you need a place to put the code.

So: GitHub.

---

You need somewhere to run it.

So: AWS. Or Vercel. Or Railway. You'll decide later.

*You won't decide later.*

---

You need a database.

Supabase. PlanetScale. Neon. You spend forty-five minutes comparing pricing tiers.
You pick one. You're not sure why.

---

You need to handle auth.
You're not writing auth from scratch.
Not again.

Auth0. Clerk. "Just use NextAuth." Someone on Reddit says just use NextAuth.
You don't just use NextAuth.

---

You need secrets management.
You need a CDN.
You need error tracking.

Doppler. Cloudflare. Sentry.

---

You need logs.
Datadog is $180 a month.
You find something cheaper.
The something cheaper goes down on a Tuesday.

---

You need CI/CD.
You need a payment processor.
You need a deployment preview environment so you can see the thing before it's live.

---

It is now Thursday.

You have not written code.

You have seventeen browser tabs open.

You have an invoice from eleven companies.

You have three Slack workspaces, two of which are support channels for tools you're not sure you still use.

---

And somewhere in the middle of configuring your third webhook —

you forget what you were building.

---

Not the technical details.

The *why.*

The thing you sat down to make at 9am on Monday.

---

*That's the bill they don't show you.*

Not the $12/month.
Not the $49/month.
Not the $180/month.

The cost of your attention.
Paid out in seventeen browser tabs
to seventeen companies
who each think they're the center of your stack.

---

RoadCode is the whole stack.

One place.
Code. Deploy. Database. Secrets. Auth. CI/CD. Logs. Previews.

One dashboard.
One invoice.
One place to go when something breaks at 2am.

---

You open one tab.

You write the thing you sat down to build.

You ship it.

---

*That's it.*

---

**RoadCode.**
*Just build the thing.*
