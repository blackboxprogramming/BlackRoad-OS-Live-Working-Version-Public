---

There's a teacher in Portland.
She sends updates to her students' families.
Field trips. Schedule changes. The thing they need to know.

She posts it.
She emails it.
She puts it in the app.
She texts the room parent.

And still, every Monday, at least three families didn't get the message.

Not because they didn't want it.
Because it went to the wrong place,
or the right place at the wrong time,
or the right place at the right time
and got buried under seventeen other things
that an algorithm decided were equally important.

---

The message was sent.

It was not delivered.

---

*There's a difference.*

---

You send. It arrives.

No feed. No algorithm. No reach percentage.
No "your post is performing below average — boost it?"

---

A message.
To the people who asked for it.

Delivered.

---

The teacher in Portland sends it once now.

The families get it.

The field trip permission slips come back.

---

*That's it.*

That's the whole thing.
