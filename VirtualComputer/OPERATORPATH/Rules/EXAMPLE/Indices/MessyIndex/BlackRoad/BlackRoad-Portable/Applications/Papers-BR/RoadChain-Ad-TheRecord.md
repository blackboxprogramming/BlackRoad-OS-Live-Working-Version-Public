# RoadChain — "The Record"
*DI/Prose Interpretation style — Brand Ad*
*60–90 seconds VO | Emotional register: controlled fury under stillness*

---

## SCRIPT

---

There is a technology company with a valuation of four billion dollars.

And somewhere,

in a folder on a hard drive you no longer have access to,

is the file where you had the idea first.

---

You remember the date.
You remember the coffee getting cold.
You remember saving it.

You don't remember — because you couldn't know — that you should have done something else.
Something more than save.

---

They will say: *"We developed this internally."*

And the room will nod.

And the slides will have timelines.

And the timelines will not include you.

---

This is not about theft.

This is about something quieter and more permanent.

This is about how history is written by whoever has the record.

---

You have the memory.

They have the receipts.

---

There's a woman in Austin.
She designed the interaction pattern.
The gesture. The swipe. The way the thing *felt* in your hand.
You've done it four thousand times since.
You've never heard her name.

She hasn't either.
Not attached to that.

She knows.

She just can't prove it.

---

*That's the gap.*

Not between what you made and what they made.

Between what happened

and what the record says happened.

---

RoadChain is provenance.

An immutable timestamp.

A cryptographic signature on your work — the moment you make it.

Before you share it.
Before you ship it.
Before you trust anyone with it.

---

Every file.
Every commit.
Every design, every document, every idea you type into a box at 11pm —

Hashed.
Sealed.
Recorded on-chain.

Not editable.
Not deniable.
Not theirs.

---

They can build on your work.
They always have.

But now the record says where it started.

---

The woman in Austin?
She has a timestamp.

Four years ago.
Eleven months before the patent.

*She was first.*

---

That's not nothing.

That's everything.

---

**RoadChain.**
*The record is the truth. Now it's yours.*
