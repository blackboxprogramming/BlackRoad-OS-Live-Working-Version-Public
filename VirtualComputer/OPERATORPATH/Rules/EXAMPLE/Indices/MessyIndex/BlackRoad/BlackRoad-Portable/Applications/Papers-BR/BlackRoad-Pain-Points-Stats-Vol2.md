## DEVELOPER BURNOUT & PRODUCTIVITY
*(RoadCode · RoadWork · OfficeRoad)*

- **52%** of developers say burnout is a key reason peers are leaving their jobs
- **23%** of developers work overtime at least 10 days per month
- Developer burnout costs employers an estimated **$300 billion/year** in lost productivity and turnover
- **58%** of engineering leaders say devs lose more than **5 hours/week** to unproductive work
- **31%** cite "time spent gathering context" as their top productivity blocker
- It takes **23 minutes** to regain deep focus after a single interruption
- Knowledge workers (including devs) switch between apps **~1,200 times per day**
- That "toggle tax" costs up to **4 hours per week** just reorienting after app switches
- **99%** of engineering leaders say the developer job has become more complex in 2024
- **72%** say it takes more than 1 month for a new dev to submit their first 3 meaningful PRs — mostly due to context-gathering
- **62%** of developers cite scope creep as a major contributor to stress and overwhelm
- Only **31%** of developers feel AI tools have actually made them more productive (despite 44% using them daily)

## SOCIAL MEDIA REACH COLLAPSE
*(BackRoad · RoadBook · OneWay)*

- Facebook organic reach: **1.2–1.37%** in 2025 — down from **16% in 2012**
- Facebook median engagement rate: **0.2%**
- For a page with 10,000 followers, a single post reaches approximately **137 people**
- Instagram organic reach: **3.5–4%** — down **12–40% year-over-year**
- TikTok organic reach: dropped from **~24% to ~10%** in two years
- Twitter/X: roughly **3%** of followers see any given post — engagement down **48%** over 2024–25
- LinkedIn company pages: **~2% organic reach**
- **$2.4 billion/year** spent by SMBs on "boosting posts" — paying to reach people who already followed them
- The average brand post on Instagram reaches **1 in 25** people who chose to follow it

## SUBSCRIPTION FATIGUE
*(OfficeRoad · RoadCode · all products)*

- Average U.S. adult thinks they spend **$60–91/month** on subscriptions
- When they actually count: the real number is **$219/month** — more than double the estimate
- **60%** of people admit to forgetting about at least one active subscription they're still paying for
- **40%** of Americans canceled at least one subscription in the past year — mostly for budget reasons
- Average household manages **7–12 active subscriptions** simultaneously
- The global subscription economy hit **$650 billion** in 2024
- **78% of adults globally** hold at least one paid subscription
- A price increase of just **$6–10/month** is enough to trigger cancellation for one-third of subscribers
- People vastly underestimate recurring spend — **the gap between perception and reality is 2–3x**

## VENDOR LOCK-IN & PLATFORM DEPENDENCY
*(All BlackRoad products — core thesis)*

- **62%** of IT buyers express concern about lock-in with digital platforms and cloud providers (BCG, 2025)
- **70%** are actively scanning for alternatives — but stay because switching is too costly
- Switching from one platform to another typically costs **2.3x–5.7x** the original implementation investment
- Cloud migrations take an average of **18–36 months** to complete
- McKinsey projects **$100 billion in wasted cloud migration spend** over three years due to complexity and lock-in
- **32% of all cloud spend is wasted** — overprovisioning, hidden egress fees, underutilized services (Flexera 2024)
- **20% of all software budgets** are wasted on unnecessary complexity — failed implementations, unused tools
- **20–30% of operational expenditure** is lost annually to inefficiency across industries
- Cloud waste can hit **50%** in organizations with poor governance
- Small businesses lose an average of **96 minutes per day** (3 weeks per year) to technology-driven inefficiency (Slack/Salesforce, 2024)

## OPEN SOURCE EXPLOITATION
*(RoadCode · RoadChain · RoadCoin)*

- **60%** of open source maintainers are unpaid for their work (Tidelift, 2024)
- **60%** have quit or seriously considered quitting their projects
- **61%** of unpaid maintainers are solo maintainers — no team, no support, no income
- **44%** of maintainers report active burnout
- The percentage of open source maintainers aged **46–65 has doubled** since 2021
- The percentage under **26 has plummeted from 25% to 10%** since 2021 — the pipeline is aging out
- **~50% of open source labor is "invisible"** — not publicly credited or compensated
- Major cloud providers and enterprises commercialize open source for billions in revenue — the majority of contributors receive nothing
- The Log4j and xz-utils vulnerabilities both traced back to **under-resourced, underfunded solo maintainers**

## MEETING OVERLOAD & REMOTE WORK DRAG
*(OfficeRoad · RoadWork · OneWay)*

- Meeting volume has increased **252%** since early 2020
- Remote workers average **25.6 meetings per week** — 80% more than in-office workers (14.2/week)
- **1 in 3 meetings is considered unnecessary** by attendees
- Senior managers spend nearly **23 hours per week** in meetings — leaving minimal time for actual work
- **71%** of employees consider meetings unproductive
- **65%** of senior managers say meetings prevent them from completing their own work
- **82%** of workers say they're expected to attend too many meetings and work overtime to compensate
- **49%** of remote professionals report significant Zoom fatigue on a weekly basis
- **92%** of workers admit to multitasking during meetings
- Follow-up task completion is **34% lower** after remote meetings than in-person ones (without tracking)
- By 2025, **22% of the U.S. workforce** will be fully remote — meeting overload is structural, not temporary

## SURVEILLANCE CAPITALISM & DATA BROKERS
*(RoadChat · CarKeys · BackRoad)*

- The global data broker industry: **$345 billion in revenue in 2024**
- **4,000+ data broker companies** operate worldwide
- Acxiom alone stores up to **10,000 attributes per consumer**
- Experian maintains a database of **1.4 billion consumer profiles** worldwide
- A complete personal data profile (financial + behavioral + psychographic) sells for **$10–$500**
- Medical and financial data commands the highest prices on both open and black markets
- **67%** of Americans feel they understand "little to nothing" about what companies do with their data (Pew Research)
- **73%** of Americans believe they have "little to no control" over how their data is used
- **75%** of people globally feel they have lost control over their personal data
- **71%** of U.S. adults are worried about government use of personal data
- The FTC found that major social media and streaming companies have engaged in **"vast surveillance"** of users — including children and teens
- Only **26 U.S. states** have enacted any data privacy law — no comprehensive federal framework exists

## SMALL BUSINESS TECH WASTE
*(RoadWork · OfficeRoad)*

- Small businesses waste **20–30%** of operational spend to inefficiency annually
- Mid-sized companies lose **$250,000–$600,000/year** to operational inefficiency
- **20%** of software budgets wasted on failed implementations and unused tools (Freshworks)
- Cloud waste ranges **21–50%** depending on company maturity
- Small business owners lose **96 minutes/day** to inefficiency — that's **3 full weeks per year**
- Most of that lost time: siloed tools, duplicate communication, searching for misplaced information
- **20% of tech spend delivers zero value** — not because the tools are bad, but because they don't connect
